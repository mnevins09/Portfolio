About Me

I am orginally from New York and moved to St Simons Island, GA 7 years ago. I am married with one child and two dogs. I graduated from Manhattan College in 2008 while earning a Bachelor of Science Degree in Business Administration with a minor in Computer Information Systems (CIS).
While at Manhattan College, I was a member of the Division I Baseball team. In 2006, we were the Metro Atlantic Athletic Conference (MAAC) champions and advanced to the regional finals in Nebraska. During regionals, we defeated the University of Nebraska and the University of San Francisco, which were the #9 and #25 teams in the nation at the time. We were eventually defeated twice by the #13 Miami Hurricanes. We were 3 wins away from advancing to the College World Series in Omaha, Nebraska. This was one of the greatest experiences of my life.
My employment experience includes 4 years in banking for various institutions, 4 years in accounting for the State of Georgia, and 3 years working for a software company, Systems and Methods, Inc., my current employer. I began as a Teller in banking and worked my way up to be an Assistant Branch Manager running my own branch. In accounting, I began as a Generalist and worked my way up to become the Accountant in our office. I currently work from home for Systems and Methods, Inc. as a Client Support Specialist. My ultimate goal is to become a Developer within my current company in the future.
